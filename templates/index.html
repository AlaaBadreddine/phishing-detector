What is phishing?

Phishing is a social engineering attack where a malicious actor tricks you into sharing credentials, installing malware, or revealing sensitive data.

Deceptive URLs

Attackers often use look-alike domains, extra subdomains, or IP addresses to make a link appear legitimate.

Urgent language

Phishing messages often pressure you to "verify", "update", or "reset" before your account is locked.

Hidden risks

Even HTTPS can be present on phishing pages — the real danger comes from the destination and its intent.

How phishing works

A phishing attack usually follows a simple chain: lure, click, capture, and compromise.

1. The lure

A message arrives that looks urgent, official, or familiar, pushing you to act quickly.

2. The fake link

A URL uses deceptive domain names, extra subdomains, or direct IP addresses to hide malicious intent.

3. The click

The user clicks the link, and the browser lands on a spoofed page that captures credentials or installs malware.

4. The compromise

Once credentials or access are stolen, the attacker can breach accounts, steal data, or spread the attack further.
